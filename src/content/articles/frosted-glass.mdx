I've been using Pop!_OS for quite some time now, And one of the things I love about it is System76 doesn’t add features to chase the latest thing often. When they do introduce something new, it usually feels intentional.

That was my first impression exactly when I updated to the latest COSMIC desktop and saw the new Frosted Glass effect.

On the surface it might seem like a cosmetic feature. Because it's just transparency and blur, right?

Well... yes and no.

## The First Impression 
As soon as I turned on the effect my desktop looked more polished immediately. The panels, notifications, menus, and launcher no longer looked like flat blocks on top of my wallpaper. Instead they blended seamlessly into the desktop and the experience was awesome. 

It reminded me of the elegance of Windows Aero and the subtle translucency of macOS, but still felt like cosmic.

The best part is that System76 didn't force it on anyone. You can adjust the blur strength to your liking or turn it off completely if you prefer the traditional look.

## Why I like it 
But it was not the blur itself that surprised me most, but rather how much personality it added to the desktop. It’s not just sitting behind the interface, it’s now part of it, and it’s a beautiful wallpaper. Notifications seem lighter. Menus don’t take over the screen. Everything feels a little more alive, without being distracting.

This gives the desktop a modern, premium look even at lower blur strengths, while still staying true to the clean and minimal philosophy Pop!_OS is known for.

Another thing I like is that the text remains readable. Some desktop environments make transparency so bad you can't even see the UI elements. COSMIC strikes a good balance between usability and beauty.

## Where it felt short
As much as I enjoy the feature, it's not perfect.

The biggest issue I noticed is that the effect practically disappears when using applications in true fullscreen mode (F11). Since the panels and desktop are hidden, the frosted glass effect no longer has anything to enhance.

It's not exactly a bug, but it does make the experience feel inconsistent. One moment the desktop looks stunning, and the next, the visual enhancement is gone entirely.

Another thing I discovered is that the effect depends heavily on your wallpaper. Some wallpapers make the blur look absolutely gorgeous, while others make it almost invisible. Darker and simpler wallpapers tend to produce the best results.
There's also the fact that this is purely a visual upgrade. It won't make you more productive, launch applications faster, or improve your workflow. If you're expecting a feature that changes how you use your computer, this isn't it.

And although modern hardware should handle it comfortably, real-time blur is still an additional graphical effect. On older systems, there may be a small performance cost compared to using completely opaque panels.

## Final thoughts 
The Frosted Glass effect isn't a revolutionary feature, and I don't think it was ever meant to be.

Instead, it makes Pop!_OS feel more refined. It's one of those subtle improvements that you don't necessarily notice after a few days but if someone turned it off, you'd probably miss it.

I also appreciate System76's approach. Rather than copying another desktop environment outright, they've implemented the feature in a way that fits COSMIC's clean design philosophy. It's tasteful, customizable, and optional.

Would I recommend enabling it?

Absolutely.

Just don't expect it to transform the way you use your computer. Think of it as the final coat of paint on an already excellent desktop experience, a small visual touch that makes using Pop!_OS feel just a little more enjoyable every single day.
